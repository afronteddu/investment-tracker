How to Read This

Borrowing limit is the hard ceiling from Central Bank rules (4× FTB salary).

Budget = mortgage + your deposit + HTB + FHS. The deposit must be at least 10% of the purchase price.

Monthly repayment is principal + interest. Budget ~20–25% of net monthly income maximum.

HTB only applies to new builds ≤€500k. It's a refund of income tax you've paid in Ireland over 4 years — if you arrived in Oct 2025, you're building this history now.

FHS is government equity — they own a % of your home and you repay when you sell or refinance. No monthly charge for first 5 years.

If you buy a €480k home and need a €48k boost (10%), FHS contributes that as ~10% equity. The gov owns 10% of future value changes. If the home rises to €600k, you owe them €60k to redeem. Trap: equity rises with property value — plan to redeem early or factor into exit strategy.
